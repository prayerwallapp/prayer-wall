# Prayer Wall — Brand Copy Reference

Single place of reference for the three core copy pieces: what Prayer Wall is, why
it exists, and why it's different. All three are locked/drafted from earlier
sessions, consolidated here for continuity. For the underlying style rules (voice
pillars, word choices to use/avoid, the scripture-selection reasoning), see the
companion doc `voice-tone-reference.md`, this file is the applied copy itself.

---

## 1. What Is Prayer Wall

*(Synthesized from already-locked hero copy and tagline, not new claims, this is
the short descriptive version for anywhere a plain explanation is needed, About
pages, pitch decks, app store listings, etc.)*

> Prayer Wall is a live prayer and praise wall for churches. Members can submit a
> prayer request or praise report from any device. Your team reviews it before it
> goes live, and approved posts appear instantly on a shared wall your whole
> congregation can see and react to, on their phones and on the big screen at
> church.

**One-line version** (tagline, currently used as the hero badge):
> The prayer & praise wall for churches everywhere.

## 2. Why We Exist

*(Locked belief statement, "Version B," currently placed pre-footer on the landing
page. This is the full version with the moderation paragraph included, per the
locked copy doc.)*

> "For where two or three gather in my name, there am I with them." — Matthew 18:20
>
> We believe in the power of collective prayer. A request held by one person is
> real. A request held by a whole congregation, prayed over by name, is a church
> showing up for one of its own.
>
> Being the church was never meant to live inside one hour on a Sunday morning.
> It's a Monday-to-Sunday way of showing up for each other. Prayer Wall exists so
> that showing up doesn't depend on being in the same room.
>
> This is for the church, wherever it gathers, and every submission is reviewed by
> your own pastoral team before it's shared, never left to an algorithm. The point
> was never the technology. It's what the technology lets a church do for each
> other.
>
> Because that's what family does. We stand together, no matter what the week
> holds.
>
> If that's the kind of church you lead, come see what Prayer Wall could look like
> for your congregation.

**Alternate version, "Version A"** (kept as an option for warmer, already-churched
contexts, uses "brothers and sisters" instead of "family"):
> "For where two or three gather in my name, there am I with them." (Matthew 18:20)
>
> We believe in the power of collective prayer. Being the church isn't only a
> Sunday morning, it's a Monday-to-Sunday way of showing up for each other. Prayer
> Wall gives your congregation a place to gather in prayer wherever the week takes
> them. Not as strangers under one roof, but as brothers and sisters, standing
> together.

**Why this verse, briefly**: Matthew 18:20 was chosen over the original source
script's Jeremiah 29:11 specifically because it's about gathering, not petition or
provision, so it reads cleanly across Baptist, Anglican, Pentecostal, charismatic,
and non-denominational traditions without leaning into a prosperity-adjacent
reading some traditions are wary of. Full reasoning in `voice-tone-reference.md`.

## 3. Why Prayer Wall (ethos / differentiation)

*(First draft, product voice rather than belief voice. Intentionally held back
from public/live use until post-launch, per Josiah's call, drafted here so it's
ready when needed.)*

> A lot of church prayer walls online are really just public forms bolted onto a
> website. Anyone can post. Anyone can read. Real names sit next to deeply personal
> requests with no one reviewing it first, and the whole thing is visible to any
> stranger who finds the page, not just your own congregation.
>
> Prayer Wall works differently. It's your church's own wall, on your church's own
> page, reviewed by your own pastoral team before anything goes live. And it's
> built to feel like a gathering, not a comment section, with reactions that let
> members know exactly who's praying for them, and a live display option that
> brings the wall into the room, not just onto a screen.

**Status note**: this piece has not been named-competitor-checked or legally
reviewed for any implied comparison claims. It doesn't name any competitor
directly (intentional), but if this is ever expanded or made more specific, revisit
the caution flagged earlier: unverified claims about how a *specific* competing
tool works (e.g. "no one reviewing it first") should stay as reasonable inference
from observed behavior, not stated as confirmed fact about their backend.

## Source material (for reference only, not for reuse as-is)

The belief statement in section 2 was adapted from a script Josiah originally
wrote for a Hillsong Church pastor's piece-to-camera promo video, built for one
church speaking in its own voice. Kept here for provenance, not intended to be
quoted or reused directly, since Prayer Wall now speaks as a platform serving many
churches, not one church's own product.

> "Jeremiah chapter twenty-nine verses eleven through to thirteen. For I know the
> plans I have for you declares the Lord. Plans to prosper you and not to harm you.
> Plans to give you a hope and a future. Then you will call on me and come and pray
> to me and I will listen to you. You will seek me and find me when you seek me
> with all your heart. God has wonderful plans for each one of us but how do we
> access those plans? One way is through prayer. In the Bible, God promises us
> that if we pray, he will hear us. If we look for him, we will find him and if we
> need help, he will rescue us. But how do we pray? Prayer is not complicated.
> Prayer is simply a conversation with God. A conversation which he has already
> started through his word. It's a conversation to which everyone is invited. When
> we pray together, it's just a bigger conversation. At Hillsong Church, we
> believe in the power of corporate prayer. God has always wanted a house of
> prayer, a praying community where everyone is welcome. That's what we want to
> be. So, why don't you come and join us and pray on our online prayer wall. You
> can submit a prayer request. You can see other prayer requests on the wall. You
> can pray for others and be notified when someone has prayed for you."
