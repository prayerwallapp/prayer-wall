# Prayer Wall — Voice & Tone Reference

Source material: adapted from a script Josiah originally wrote for a Hillsong Church
pastor's piece-to-camera promo video. Reworked for Prayer Wall as a platform serving
many churches across denominations, not one church speaking for itself.

---

## Belief statement — two locked options

Both are approved and ready to use. Pick per context (see "Where this lives" below),
or A/B test them once there's traffic to test with.

### Option A — "brothers and sisters"
> *"For where two or three gather in my name, there am I with them." (Matthew 18:20)*
>
> *We believe in the power of collective prayer. Being the church isn't only a Sunday
> morning, it's a Monday-to-Sunday way of showing up for each other. Prayer Wall gives
> your congregation a place to gather in prayer wherever the week takes them. Not as
> strangers under one roof, but as brothers and sisters, standing together.*

Warmer, more explicitly biblical-family language. Strong across most traditions on
Josiah's target list, but "brothers and sisters" can read as dated or overly-religious
to some readers. Good fit for church-facing contexts (onboarding, admin dashboard,
communications to pastors) where the audience is already bought into church language.

### Option B — "that's what family does" (current default/preferred)
> *"For where two or three gather in my name, there am I with them." (Matthew 18:20)*
>
> *We believe in the power of collective prayer. Being the church isn't only a Sunday
> morning, it's a Monday-to-Sunday way of showing up for each other. Prayer Wall gives
> your congregation a place to gather in prayer. Because that's what family does. We
> stand together, no matter what the week holds.*

Same theology, softer landing. Drops "brothers and sisters" for "family" without the
gendered-sibling framing. Slightly more accessible to a colder/broader audience (social
ads, first-time visitors to the landing page). Bookends nicely: "Monday-to-Sunday" early,
"no matter what the week holds" at the close.

**Default to Option B for public-facing / cold-traffic contexts** (landing page, paid
and organic social, first-touch anything). Option A is fine for warmer, already-churched
contexts if it fits better there.

## Scripture approach (resolved)

- Yes to quoting scripture at the platform level — this was explicitly discussed and
  Josiah pushed back on avoiding it. The platform exists *because* of a belief about
  collective prayer, and that belief can be stated plainly.
- Verse selection must work across denominations Josiah is targeting: Baptist,
  Anglican, charismatic, evangelical, non-denominational. Matthew 18:20 was chosen
  specifically because it's about **gathering**, not petition/provision — it sidesteps
  prosperity-adjacent readings some traditions are wary of (this is why Jeremiah 29:11,
  the original Hillsong script's verse, was not carried forward as the platform's verse).
- No in-depth theological interpretation in platform copy. State the belief, don't
  exegete it. Doctrine stays with each individual church, through their own labels
  and their own pastor's voice — Prayer Wall's job is believing in the *practice* of
  visible, shared prayer, not teaching *what to believe* about prayer.
- Translation is an open choice, not yet locked. NIV (Biblica) allows short quotes like
  this freely with attribution, no license required. KJV is public domain and sidesteps
  the permission question entirely. Either works at this length.

## Voice pillars

1. **Collective, not solitary** — talk about the church, the congregation, gathered
   prayer. Personal devotional life isn't the subject; the layer on top of it is.
2. **Gathering, not seeing** — corrected mid-conversation. Early drafts used
   visibility/exposure language ("a whole church seeing it," "whisper vs. seen").
   That reads as privacy-invasive and slightly mystical. Reframed around participation
   and belonging instead: "gather," "showing up for," "stand together." This applies
   everywhere going forward, not just the belief statement — watch for "seeing/visible"
   language creeping back into landing copy, social captions, or FAQ answers.
3. **Practice, not doctrine** — see scripture section above.
4. **Plain, not preachy** — short sentences, no altar-call cadence, no crescendo
   building. State conviction once, clearly, and stop.
5. **Universal, not sectarian** — scripture and language chosen so a Baptist, an
   Anglican, a Pentecostal, and a non-denom pastor all recognize the same sentence
   without it feeling like someone else's dialect.

## Words to avoid / words that work

| Avoid | Why | Use instead |
|---|---|---|
| whisper | Frames private prayer as secretive, almost mystical, when spoken about in contrast to shared prayer | (cut entirely, don't contrast with a negative framing of personal prayer) |
| "a whole church seeing it" / "seen" | Reads as exposure/surveillance, privacy turn-off | gather, gathering, come together |
| brothers and sisters (situational, not banned) | Can read as dated/overly-religious to some readers, especially cold audiences | family, standing together, showing up for each other |
